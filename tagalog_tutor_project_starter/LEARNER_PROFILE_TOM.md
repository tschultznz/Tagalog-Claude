# Learner Profile: Tom

## Language background

- Native German speaker.
- Fluent English speaker.
- Adult learner of Tagalog.
- Has substantial prior exposure through Glossika.
- Has completed structured work across earlier modules and current Stage 4B.

## Cognitive style

Tom is analytical and learns best through:

- structure
- root-family maps
- pattern contrasts
- controlled variation
- practical scenes
- explicit but compact explanations
- understanding why a form is selected

He often processes Tagalog in two passes:

1. Decide what he wants to say.
2. Construct the correct Tagalog form.

This slows production and listening, but structure is currently his bridge when retrieval fails.

## Learning preferences

Tom prefers:

- production before full answers
- hints below drills
- concise corrections
- natural spoken Tagalog
- practical use
- messy speaking
- explicit distinctions such as actor versus target
- writing his attempt before the corrected form
- explanations that are stable and structurally coherent

Tom dislikes:

- full answer keys before attempting
- unexplained chunks
- vague claims such as "this is just more natural"
- paradigm dumps
- explanations that change midstream
- repeated same-session drilling without later review
- treating recognition as mastery
- losing his previous Glossika investment

## Current strengths

- practical transport scenes
- basic shopping and store exchanges
- home and food scenes
- basic health descriptions
- `dapat akong` versus `dapat kong`
- several actor-track and target-track contrasts
- messy production with support
- listening recognition for short dialogues

## Current weak points

- delayed retrieval across days
- pronoun and clitic order
- switching between actor and target tracks
- base form after modals versus contemplated form outside modals
- similar-looking verb families
- spelling of low-frequency body and health words
- aspect when multiple tracks are available
- recalling older modules after long gaps

## Important learner observation

Tom does not want structure removed.

The system should use structure as a fallback while repeated retrieval gradually makes high-frequency forms automatic.

## Motivation

Tom is highly motivated when he sees genuine recall and can use Tagalog in messy, realistic scenes.

He is interested in:

- streaks
- XP
- mastery maps
- personal-best scores
- module boss scenes
- delayed-review success
- gamification tied to actual learning quality

He is not primarily motivated by decorative rewards or generic leaderboards.
