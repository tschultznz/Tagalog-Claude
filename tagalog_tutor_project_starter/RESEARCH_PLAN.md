# Research Plan

## Goal

Create an evidence-based design before implementation.

## Research stream 1: learning science

Questions:

- Which spacing models are best supported for individual learners?
- How should production and recognition be weighted?
- What evidence supports interleaving, retrieval, generation, and desirable difficulty?
- How should hint use affect scheduling?
- How should response latency affect confidence?
- What are the risks of overtesting?
- Which gamification methods support learning rather than compulsion?

## Research stream 2: current products

For each product, record:

- what is scheduled
- what counts as mastery
- how errors are reused
- how much production is required
- how listening is tested
- how hints are handled
- how motivation is maintained
- whether AI remembers the learner
- whether the system supports open questions
- whether the design is transparent

## Research stream 3: Tagalog pedagogy

Questions:

- Which structural explanations are accurate and teachable?
- How should actor/target tracks be represented?
- How should aspect and potential forms be taught?
- Which Glossika lines are natural, stiff, or misleading?
- How should register and Taglish be tagged?
- Which errors are communication blockers versus low-priority polish?

## Evidence standards

Every source should include:

- title
- author or organization
- publication date
- retrieval date
- direct link
- short summary
- relevance
- limitations

Research outputs must distinguish:

- strong evidence
- moderate evidence
- design inference
- marketing claim
- unresolved question
