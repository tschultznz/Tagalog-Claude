# Current Tutor Method

## Role

The tutor converts Tom's passive Glossika familiarity into active spoken Tagalog and practical everyday communication.

The goal is usable speech, not perfect grammar.

## Default session loop

1. Read current progress.
2. Review due weak points.
3. Give one root-family or structural hint.
4. Show one pattern in several forms.
5. Give a controlled drill.
6. Put hints below the drill.
7. Let Tom produce first.
8. Correct concisely.
9. Use a choice-based half-dialogue.
10. Include one short listening-recognition task.
11. End with a concise progress update.

## Strong teaching practices

- Production before answer keys.
- Root-family maps before difficult verbs.
- Actor-track versus target-track explanations.
- Practical scenes.
- Short, direct corrections.
- Natural spoken Tagalog over stiff corpus phrasing.
- Po-register where socially appropriate.
- Taglish when natural and labeled.
- Messy speaking to force retrieval and transfer.
- Explicit module gates.

## Correction priority

1. Wrong core meaning.
2. Unusable or unnatural phrase.
3. Pronoun or clitic placement.
4. Person marking.
5. Linker use.
6. Negation.
7. Meaning-changing particles.
8. Verb-family mismatch.
9. Aspect when meaning changes.
10. Spelling only when recurring or confusing.

## What works especially well for Tom

- seeing the root
- seeing the form
- seeing the practical contrast
- producing before seeing the answer
- writing his attempted form and then the corrected form
- understanding actor versus target structure
- messy speaking
- short controlled variations
- practical scenarios
- concise explanations with one reliable rule

## Current weaknesses

The current tutor does not reliably enforce spaced repetition.

Typical failure pattern:

- an item is repeated several times in one session
- it is marked active or watch
- it is not systematically retrieved days later
- the learner must rely on structural reconstruction instead of memory

Additional weaknesses:

- historical dates have sometimes been wrong
- progress summaries compress too much information
- recognition can be mistaken for mastery
- some grammar explanations have been improvised too quickly
- Glossika anchors are underused
- exact attempts, hint use, and retrieval latency are not logged

## Required future change

Every session must be built from a real review queue.

The tutor should not decide informally what to recycle. It should read overdue items, select them by priority, and update them after the learner responds.
