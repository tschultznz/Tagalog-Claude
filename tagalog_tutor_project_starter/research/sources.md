# Sources

All sources retrieved **2026-06-23** (US web search) unless noted. Each entry: claim relevance, evidence strength, and limitation. Strength = how much weight the design puts on it, not the prestige of the venue. Where findings came via search summaries of primary papers, the primary paper is named and the summary is the access path; treat exact effect sizes as approximate pending direct read.

## Learning science

1. **Cepeda, Vul, Rohrer, Wixted & Pashler (2008), "Spacing Effects in Learning: A Temporal Ridgeline of Optimal Retention," *Psychological Science* 19(11):1095–1102.** — Optimal inter-study gap scales with retention interval; ~20–40% of a 1-week target down to ~5–10% of a 1-year target. **STRONG** (n>1,350). Limit: lab facts, not L2 grammar. https://laplab.ucsd.edu/articles/Cepeda%20et%20al%202008_psychsci.pdf
2. **Roediger & Karpicke (2006), testing effect; Rowland (2014) meta g≈0.50; Adesope et al. (2017) meta g≈0.61; Pan & Rickard (2018) transfer d≈0.40; Yang et al. (2021) classroom g≈0.50.** — Retrieval > restudy, advantage grows with delay. **STRONG.** Access via review summaries. Limit: effect sizes vary by test format. https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3983480/ ; https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40594-024-00468-5
3. **Generation effect / errorful generation / pretesting** — self-generated (even failed-then-corrected) answers beat reading; 300+ experiments, 3 meta-analyses, applies to vocab/translation. **STRONG.** Limit: requires corrective feedback to realize the failed-attempt benefit. https://www.structural-learning.com/post/generation-effect-active-learning ; https://link.springer.com/article/10.3758/BF03202628
4. **Bjork & Bjork — desirable difficulties; storage vs retrieval strength; interleaving (Rohrer & Taylor 63% vs 20% delayed); "myth that blocking helps."** — Interleaving/spacing depress practice but raise retention/transfer; learners misjudge this. **STRONG** (concept), **MODERATE** (size varies by domain). https://bjorklab.psych.ucla.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/13/2016/04/EBjork_RBjork_2011.pdf ; https://bera-journals.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/rev3.3266
5. **Assistance dilemma / hint dependence** — Koedinger & Aleven and ITS literature; over-used bottom-out hints correlate with worse learning; target help at ~0.5 success. **MODERATE.** Limit: mostly math/programming tutors, not L2. https://jedm.educationaldatamining.org/index.php/JEDM/article/view/450 ; https://arxiv.org/pdf/2010.04124
6. **Gamification + Self-Determination Theory in language learning** — gamification can serve competence/autonomy/relatedness; immediate feedback + progress + adaptive challenge strongest; points/badges/leaderboards mixed. **MODERATE.** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11163042/ ; https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/psychology/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2024.1295709/full
7. **Streaks / dark patterns critique (Duolingo)** — loss aversion + habit loops; possible anxiety/burnout; autonomy tension. **CONTESTED** (largely essays/blogs, not RCTs; treat as ethical caution, not finding). https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/15554120251319173 ; https://theeconomyofmeaning.com/2025/08/25/a-critical-look-at-how-to-make-learning-as-addictive-as-social-media-a-ted-talk-about-duolingo/
8. **Corrective-feedback timing in SLA — systematic review (Frontiers 2023, PMC9995700).** — Theory/learner-preference favor immediate; empirical mixed; vocab may benefit from delay more than grammar. **CONTESTED/MODERATE.** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9995700/

## Spaced-repetition algorithms / products

9. **FSRS — open-spaced-repetition (Jarrett Ye).** Difficulty/Stability/Retrievability; power-law forgetting; default in Anki (native since v23.10, Nov 2023; FSRS-6 2025); ~20–30% fewer reviews at equal retention. **MODERATE** (well-documented; "best free/open," not "provably optimal"). https://github.com/open-spaced-repetition/free-spaced-repetition-scheduler ; https://faqs.ankiweb.net/what-spaced-repetition-algorithm
10. **SM-2 / SuperMemo (Woźniak).** Origin of expanding-interval SRS; powers Anki(legacy)/Memrise/Quizlet/others; latest SM-line proprietary. FSRS-vs-SuperMemo superiority **CONTESTED** by SuperMemo-aligned authors. https://supermemo.guru/wiki/FSRS
11. **Duolingo Birdbrain** — deep net predicting per-exercise correctness, updated daily on ~1.25B exercises (with CMU); real-time difficulty/timing tuning; Max adds AI speaking. **MARKETING/OBSERVED.** https://chiefaiofficer.com/duolingos-ai-strategy-fuels-51-user-growth-and-1b-revenue/ ; https://www.buildmvpfast.com/blog/ai-learning-personalization-duolingo-ai-driven-lessons-2026
12. **Glossika method** — sentence-level SRS, ~15–20 reps/sentence/year, globally-tuned difficulty, audio-first mass repetition. **MARKETING/OBSERVED.** https://ai.glossika.com/blog/the-glossika-method ; https://help.glossika.com/en/articles/8599638
13. **Babbel** — daily spaced review before new lesson; AI for smart review + pronunciation. **OBSERVED** (third-party reviews). https://www.icanlearn.com/busuu-vs-babbel/
14. **Busuu Conversations** (since late 2024) — AI speaking partner, **no mid-turn correction, end-of-session feedback summary**, CEFR-leveled. **OBSERVED.** https://blog.busuu.com/new-conversations-release/ ; https://www.busuu.com/en/languages/language-learning-with-busuu-conversations
15. **Speak** — Learn→Practice→Real-Conversation loop; OpenAI-powered tutor + on-demand grammar Q&A + custom lessons; speaking-first. **MARKETING.** https://www.speak.com/ ; https://openai.com/index/speak-connor-zwick/
16. **Memrise** (mems + native-speaker video), **LingQ** (comprehensible input + known/unknown tagging), **Praktika/Loora/Gliglish** (AI role-play + post-session reports). **OBSERVED/MARKETING.** https://www.mindomax.com/anki-vs-memrise ; https://gliglish.com/ ; https://www.loora.com/

## Tagalog linguistics

17. **"Tagalog grammar," Wikipedia** (tertiary pointer) — voice/trigger system; actor (-um-, mag-, ma-, mang-) vs patient (-in, i-, -an); aspect = contemplated/imperfective/perfective + infinitive; second-position clitics. **VERIFIED** (consistent with Schachter & Otanes; Kroeger). https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tagalog_grammar
18. **TagalogNow — "Verb Focus System Explained (It's Not Active vs Passive)."** Pedagogical confirmation that focus ≠ active/passive and that patient focus is common/unmarked. **NATURALNESS/VERIFIED.** https://www.tagalognow.com/learn/verb-focus-system-explained/
19. **`magpa-` causative; `magpatingin` = "have oneself examined" (root *tingin*).** Confirmed across multiple sources incl. McFarland, "Tagalog Causative Verbs" (PSSC). Contemplated = `magpapatingin`. **VERIFIED.** https://pssc.org.ph/wp-content/pssc-archives/Philippine%20Journal%20of%20Linguistics/1984-1985/04_Tagalog%20Causative%20Verbs.pdf ; https://koalakouala.cloud/en/magpa-verbs-usage/
20. **Aspect morphology — contemplated (CV-reduplication, irrealis), imperfective (reduplication+realis), perfective (realis, no reduplication), infinitive (bare).** UH Mānoa Filipino grammar; MacLachlan, "Morphosyntax of Tagalog." **VERIFIED.** Note: aspect can co-occur with `dapat` in some natural constructions → the "base form after dapat" rule is **TEACHING-RULE**, not absolute law. https://www.hawaii.edu/filipino/Grammar_Topics/Grammar_2-1.html
21. **Second-position (Wackernagel) clitics; linker -ng/na; pronoun + particle ordering** — Kroeger, "Clitics and clause structure in Tagalog"; Schachter & Otanes. **VERIFIED.** https://www.diu.edu/documents/Kroeger-Clitics-Llamzon-online.pdf

## Evidence-handling notes
- Vendor pages (11, 12, 15, 16) describe *intended* behavior; independent reviews (13, 14, 16) and the project's own knowledge of Glossika corroborate where possible. Where only marketing exists, the design does not depend on the claim being optimal — only on the *mechanic* being real and copyable.
- Several search results surfaced future-dated or low-credibility domains; those were **excluded** from citation. The list above is restricted to primary papers, established references, official product pages, or peer-reviewed/edu sources.
- Effect sizes are as reported in secondary summaries; the design's dependence on any single number is low (it depends on *direction*, which is robust).
