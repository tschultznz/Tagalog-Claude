TAGALOG CURRICULUM — STAGE 4B REBUILD
Version: v2, Claude-patched
Last updated: 2026-05-09

PURPOSE
Stage 4B is a deliberate bridge before Stage 5.
It expands Tom's everyday active vocabulary and real-life conversational control without abandoning structure.

Stage 4B exists because Tom needs:
- broader daily vocabulary
- colors and description words
- numbers, money, and time
- po-register and particles
- listening recognition
- natural Taglish awareness
- self-introduction and being asked about himself
- structured practice that slowly becomes messier

STAGE 4B METHOD
Each module uses:
- root-family or concept hint first
- one main pattern in multiple forms
- choice-based half-dialogues
- English intent hints below prompts
- no full answer key before production
- concise correction after production
- one short listening micro
- date-tagged progress update

STAGE 4B SESSION LOAD
Normal session:
- 1 due weak point
- 8 to 12 old phrases or micro-frames
- 3 to 6 new lexical items or chunks
- 1 concept/root-family hint
- 4 to 8 controlled prompts
- 3 to 5 half-dialogues
- 1 listening-recognition micro
- 1 short original line or scene when stable

SPACED REVIEW
Every session should check progress.txt for:
- D3 items: last seen 3+ days ago
- D7 items: last seen 7+ days ago
- D14 items: last seen 14+ days ago
- HIGH priority weak points

CORPUS NATURALNESS
Use the corpus as the familiarity base.
If a corpus line is stilted, teach the natural spoken target and optionally mention the corpus version in one line.

TAGLISH NOTE
Stage 4B teaches natural code-switching when useful.
Taglish is not a failure; it is part of real Filipino speech.
But it must be controlled and labeled:
- [TAGLISH-NATURAL]
- [PURE TAGALOG OPTION]
- [AVOID: broken mix]

MODULE 52. DESCRIBING THINGS I: SIZE, TEMPERATURE, OPEN/CLOSED, NEW/OLD
==================================================

Scene:
Home, room, shop, café, service counter.

Main goals:
- describe things and situations
- learn basic contrasts
- use adjective sentences naturally
- avoid textbook stiffness

Core vocabulary:
- malaki / maliit
- mainit / malamig
- bago / luma
- bukas / sarado
- madilim / maliwanag
- malinis / madumi

Core frames:
- Malaki ang kuwarto.
- Maliit ang bag.
- Mainit ang kape.
- Malamig ang tubig.
- Bukas ba ang tindahan?
- Sarado na ba ito?
- Mainit dito sa kuwarto.
- Madilim dito.

Naturalness note:
Prefer "Mainit dito sa kuwarto" or "Mainit sa kuwartong ito" over stilted "Mainit sa kuwarto na ito."

Grammar/control:
Predicate adjective:
- Mainit ang kape.
Modifier adjective:
- mainit na kape
- malaking kuwarto

Do not over-theorize. Teach as two useful frames.

Listening micro:
2-3 lines with mainit/malamig/bukas/sarado and one particle like na/pa.

Advancement:
Tom can describe 5 objects/situations and ask 3 yes/no adjective questions.

MODULE 53.5. NUMBERS, MONEY, TIME, AND COUNTING
==================================================

Scene:
Shopping, time, appointments, transport, ordering.

Why inserted:
Numbers were too scattered. They are a hidden bottleneck for money, time, and everyday logistics.

Core targets:
- native counting: isa, dalawa, tatlo...
- Spanish-derived forms for time/money recognition where common
- ilan vs ilang
- isang kape / dalawang tao
- alas-singko / alas-otso y media
- magkano
- piso, barya, sukli

Core frames:
- Magkano po ito?
- Ilang tao?
- Ilang kape?
- Anong oras?
- Alas-singko.
- May sukli po ba?
- Dalawang kape po.
- Isang tubig at isang kape.

Teaching note:
Do not attempt total number mastery in one session.
Teach numbers in small practical scenes:
- ordering
- price
- time
- people count

Listening micro:
Short café or driver exchange with price/time.

Advancement:
Tom can ask price, say simple quantities, understand simple time, and count people/things in small numbers.

Core vocabulary:

Core frames:

- Ano ang gusto mong inumin?

- May kanin ba?
- Wala nang kanin.
- Pakiabot mo nga 'yung tubig.
- Paki-order nito. [TAGLISH-NATURAL]

Grammar/control:
- gusto ko ng + thing
- gusto kong + action
- may/wala availability
- paki- requests

Naturalness:
- "Pakiabot mo nga 'yung tubig" is more natural than bare "Pakiabot nga" when the object is clear.
- "Paki-order nito" is Taglish-natural. Pure option: "Puwede mo bang i-order ito?"

Advancement:
Tom can order basic food/drink, ask what someone wants, and request table items.

- Ito lang.
- Sige.

- Sandali lang po.
- Puwede po ba akong magtanong?

Advancement:
